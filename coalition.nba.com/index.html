The Stakes

Good public policy changes lives.

And the numbers speak for themselves.

1 in 3 Americans

1 in 3 Americans has a criminal record, which can result in over 40K barriers limiting access to jobs, housing, education, voting, rights, and opportunities.

Source: National Reentry Resource Center

1 in 5 fatalities

1 in 5 fatal police shootings involve a person in mental health crisis.

Source: The Appeal

46,000 lives

46,000 Americans die from gun violence every year—an average of over 125 lives lost per day. This is a public health epidemic that disproportionately impacts youth of color

Source: Giffords Law Center

32,000 children

The U.S. confines kids at a rate 2X the global average: nearly 32,000 on any given day.

Source: Prison Policy Initiative

1 in 3 voters

In any given U.S. election, 1 in 3 eligible voters doesn’t cast a ballot. Less than half of young people ages 18-29 voted in the 2024 election.

Sources: Pew Research Center, Tufts CIRCLE

$557 billion

Gun violence costs our economy an estimated $557 billion annually, on top of the incalculable cost of lives lost and quality of life harmed.

Source: Harvard Medical School

The Coalition members care about different issues, but what binds us is a shared desire to fight for everyone to be treated with dignity, no matter their race, education, religion, sexual orientation, or economic situation.
